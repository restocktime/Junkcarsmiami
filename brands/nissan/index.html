The CVT Transmission Problem - Miami's Nissan Epidemic

‚öôÔ∏è Understanding Nissan's CVT Crisis

Nissan's Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) has become a major issue for Miami owners. Starting in 2013, Nissan installed CVT transmissions in most models, leading to widespread failures:

What is CVT?
  • Continuously Variable Transmission - no fixed gear ratios
  • Designed for better fuel economy
  • Smoother acceleration in theory
  • Lower manufacturing costs for Nissan
Common CVT Problems in Miami Heat
  • Overheating: Miami heat accelerates CVT fluid breakdown
  • Shuddering: Jerky acceleration, especially from stops
  • Slipping: Loss of power transfer to wheels
  • Complete Failure: Total transmission replacement needed
  • Whining Noise: High-pitched sounds during acceleration
Affected Model Years
  • Altima: 2013-2018 (worst years), ongoing issues
  • Sentra: 2013-2019, continued problems
  • Rogue: 2014-2020, frequent failures
  • Pathfinder: 2013-2016, major issues
  • Maxima: 2016+, some problems
