Important: We strictly follow all Florida laws regarding vehicle purchases without titles. We never engage in illegal activities and ensure all transactions are fully compliant with state regulations.

Legal Ways to Sell Your Car Without Title in Florida

üèõÔ∏è Option 1: Derelict Vehicle Process (Form HSMV 82137)

For vehicles that qualify as "derelict motor vehicles" under Florida Statute 319.30:

  • Vehicle must be 10+ years old
  • Vehicle must be valued at under $1,000
  • We complete Form HSMV 82137 at time of purchase
  • This permanently cancels the vehicle's ability to be titled again
  • Used for true junk vehicles destined for scrap/parts

This is the most common method for older junk cars in Miami.

üìÑ Option 2: Duplicate Title Process (Form HSMV 82101)

For vehicles that don't qualify as derelict:

  • Owner must obtain duplicate title first
  • We can guide you through the HSMV process
  • Required for newer vehicles or those worth more than $1,000
  • Takes 7-10 business days to receive duplicate
  • We'll hold your quote during this process

We provide step-by-step assistance with this process.

Frequently Asked Questions - No Title Cars

Is it legal to buy cars without titles in Florida?

Yes, but only through specific legal processes. For vehicles 10+ years old valued under $1,000, we use Form HSMV 82137. For other vehicles, the owner must obtain a duplicate title first using Form HSMV 82101.

How long does the no-title process take?

For derelict vehicles (qualifying cars), we can complete the sale same day. For duplicate title situations, it takes 7-10 business days to get the duplicate from HSMV, but we'll hold your quote.

Will you pay less for a car without title?

No! We pay the same amount whether you have a title or not. The legal process doesn't reduce your vehicle's value to us.

What if I inherited a car but other family members might have claims?

We can help navigate multi-heir situations. Generally, we need documented proof of your right to sell (estate documents, affidavit of heirship, etc.) or consent from other heirs.
