Can You Legally Sell a Car Without Title in Florida?

Yes, you can sell a car without title in Florida, but the process requires specific documentation and varies depending on your situation. Florida law provides several legal alternatives for vehicle owners who have lost their title or never received one.

Situations Where You Might Not Have Title

Lost or Stolen Title

Most common situation - you had the title but it was lost, destroyed, or stolen.

Solution: Apply for duplicate title from Florida DHSMV

Inherited Vehicle

Vehicle was inherited from family member and title was never transferred.

Solution: Probate documentation or affidavit of inheritance

Lien Situations

Bank or finance company holds title due to outstanding loan.

Solution: Lien release documentation or payoff arrangements

Never Received Title

Purchased vehicle but seller never provided proper title transfer.

Solution: Bill of sale and VIN verification process

Legal Alternatives to Title in Florida

1. Duplicate Title Application (Form HSMV 82101)

If you previously had title but lost it, this is your best option:

  • Complete Form HSMV 82101 - Application for Duplicate Title
  • Provide valid Florida driver's license or ID
  • Pay $75.25 fee for duplicate title
  • Process takes 5-10 business days
  • Must be registered owner to apply

2. Affidavit of Ownership (Form HSMV 82137)

For vehicles without liens worth less than $1,000:

  • Vehicle must be 10+ years old or worth under $1,000
  • Complete notarized affidavit stating facts of ownership
  • Provide bill of sale or other ownership evidence
  • No liens can exist on the vehicle
  • Acceptable for most junk car buyers

3. Court-Ordered Title

When other methods aren't available:

  • Petition local county court for ownership determination
  • Provide all available ownership documentation
  • Court issues order establishing ownership
  • Most comprehensive legal protection
  • Takes 30-60 days but very secure

4. Bonded Title Process

For higher-value vehicles without clear ownership:

  • Purchase surety bond for 1.5x vehicle value
  • Bond protects against future ownership claims
  • Required for vehicles worth over $1,000
  • Bond remains active for 3 years
  • More expensive but legally comprehensive

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to get a duplicate title in Florida?

A duplicate title costs $75.25 in Florida. Additional fees may apply for expedited processing or if you need a lien added or removed.

How long does the duplicate title process take?

Standard processing takes 5-10 business days. Expedited service is available for additional fees, reducing the time to 2-3 days.

Can I sell a car without title if there's a lien?

Yes, but you must work with the lienholder to obtain a lien release or arrange for loan payoff as part of the sale transaction.

What if the car is in my deceased parent's name?

Inherited vehicles require probate documentation or an affidavit of inheritance. We can help guide you through this process.

Is it legal to buy cars without titles?

Yes, licensed auto dealers can legally purchase vehicles without titles when proper alternative documentation is provided.
