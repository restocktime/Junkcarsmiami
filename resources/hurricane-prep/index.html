Pre-Hurricane Vehicle Preparation

‚úÖ 72-Hour Hurricane Checklist for Your Vehicle

72 Hours Before Storm

  • Fill gas tank completely - stations close early
  • Check tire pressure and spare tire
  • Test battery and charging system
  • Document vehicle condition with photos
  • Review insurance policy coverage
  • Identify evacuation routes and parking options

48 Hours Before Storm

  • Secure covered parking if possible
  • Remove or secure external accessories
  • Stock emergency kit in vehicle
  • Charge all electronic devices
  • Park on highest available ground
  • Avoid underground parking in flood zones

24 Hours Before Storm

  • Move vehicle to final safe location
  • Remove valuable items from car
  • Take final condition photos
  • Record VIN and license plate clearly
  • Ensure you have car keys with you
  • Do not park under trees or power lines

Types of Hurricane Vehicle Damage

üåä Flood Damage (Most Common)

Minor Water Damage (Under Dashboard)

  • Carpet and seat dampness
  • Minor electrical issues
  • Musty odors developing
  • Repair Cost: $2,000-$8,000
  • Often Repairable

Major Flood Damage (Dashboard Level)

  • Engine compartment flooding
  • Major electrical system damage
  • Transmission and brake fluid contamination
  • Repair Cost: $8,000-$20,000
  • Usually Totaled

Complete Submersion

  • Engine block water damage
  • Complete electrical failure
  • Structural damage potential
  • Total Loss - Not Repairable

  • Body Damage: Dents, scratches, paint damage
  • Roof Damage: Hail, falling objects

Working with Insurance After Hurricane Damage

üìã Step-by-Step Insurance Claims Process

1

Contact Insurance Immediately

  • Call within 24-48 hours of damage
  • Have policy number and VIN ready
  • Provide detailed damage description
  • Request expedited claim processing
2

Document Everything

  • Take extensive photos from all angles
  • Video walk-around showing damage
  • Keep receipts for towing/storage
  • Document pre-storm condition if available
3

Professional Inspection

  • Wait for insurance adjuster appointment
  • Be present during inspection if possible
  • Point out all damage, including minor issues
  • Get copy of adjuster's report
4

Settlement Decision

  • Review total loss vs. repair decision
  • Understand settlement calculation
  • Negotiate if offer seems low
  • Consider salvage value retention
